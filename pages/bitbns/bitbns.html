Challenge 1
Optimizing the landing page for “Beginner” traders.
Understanding the user
Naturally, the biggest pain point of any user starting investing or trading for the first time is “I don’t know what I’m doing. I don’t want to lose my hard earned money”. The fundamental frustration of this group of users is lack of financial education. Investopedia found out that
Only 37% of affluent millennials feel knowledgeable about investing at all.
High-income millennials who feel knowledgeable about investing are 5X more likely (73% vs. 14%) to feel very confident in their ability to make their own financial decisions.

The second frustration that is common to both the experienced trader and the beginner is “Can I trust this platform?” After the trust is established, the initial question of any consumer would be “Why bitbns? How is it better than the other platforms? How much does it cost to start”
Existing market solutions
Many trading applications targeted at beginners like IQoptions and Olymptrade capitalize on this frustration of the user. They promise step by step learning guides and ready made strategies that will help them become a profitable trader. They’ve run several marketing campaigns with million+ views on instagram based on this very idea validating that financial education is afterall the main pain point of the user.
How I’ve planned to tackle the problem.
1. Establish Bitbns as a legitimate trading platform (Showing the trades and currencies available) without intimidating the newer user.
2. Answer the broader questions that both users have before creating a free account. (Is it trustworthy?, How much will it cost me?, Customer Support)
3. Tackle the primary need of the beginner trader by offering classes and strategies (Rebranding existing youtube videos as bitbns academy)
4. Highlight other lesser known features of the platform (mainly targeted at the experienced traders)
5. Establish social proof with user testimonials.
6. Final Call to Action.

Challenge 2
Improving KYC onboarding
Introduction
Considering that users can still trade with a smaller amount without KYC verification and that newer traders have a lot of anxiety when it comes to any information intensive operations, it is crucial to note that :
  • KYC must not be made compulsory.
  • We must highlight the benefits of KYC verification.
  • Comforting the user that their account has been created and that KYC is just an extra step
  • Not creating obtrusive ux for the user
  • Prompting KYC verification when a feature that requires KYC verification is attempted.
  • Must be broken down into steps that the user can resume and continue whenever they want.
Primary assumption
People have easier access to their physical documents easier than digital copies of them.
Approach
Shifting pressure on the backend for verification and less pressure on the user by not making it compulsory for the user to input details. Rather, making an AI/Bitbns representative identify the details from the picture.
Scope for optimization
  • If a majority of photos taken result in unidentifiable information, the input fields can be made compulsory for the user, along with the picture.
  • If a small minority of the IDs are unidentifiable, the user can be prompted on the homescreen to type out the details and take a better picture on only the unidentifiable document.
  • If there is an excess amount of work on the backend, the input fields can be made compulsory to reduce pressure on the backend
  • Automatically identifying the id number from the picture using AI. Services like jumio allow for such features. Jumio can be seen implemented on the Astropay App
